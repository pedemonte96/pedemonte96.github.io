Personal Profile

Hello, I am Martí! I consider myself a very curious person, who always wants to try and learn new things. I believe it is the scientist in me that makes me want to find answers to unsolved questions, applying rigorous thinking and learning new abilities in the journey. I also think that in order to grow, both professionally and personally, one needs to be challenged. That is why I consistently look for different problems that could enhance my problem-solving skills. A simple example would be building this webpage, but a more complex one would be studying abroad at ETH Zürich. The latter turned out to be an incredible experience, and makes me want to encourage everyone to step out their comfort zone, for instance, working or studying away from their home town.

In my free time I like to go to concerts with my close ones, read a good book, go to the gym and do sports with good company and travel and discover new places, cultures and people.

Work Experience

March 2021 - January 2022

Undergraduate Research Assistant

UB Universitat de Barcelona

Development of a ML software to assist in prescribing treatments for the fertility company Fertypharm.

The project was a colaboration between the Bosch i Gimpera Foundation and the Department of Electronics and Biomedical Engineering of the Universitat de Barcelona, and supervised by Dr. Manel López. We developed a software that used machine learning techniques, such as classifiers and regressors, to predict the most effective treatment to maximize the chances of a patient having a positive fetal heart rate after in vitro fertilization. The main programming language used in this project was Python.

December 2019 - July 2021

Undergraduate Research Assistant

UB Universitat de Barcelona

Assembly of an apparatus to produce and detect polarization-entangled photons.

The project took place in the Department of Quantum Physics and Astrophysics of the Universitat de Barcelona, under the supervision of Dr. José Maria Gómez. The setup was based on a paper by D. Dehlinger and M. W. Mitchell, that aimed to violate the CHSH Bell inequality as the authors did in a previous paper. We designed and built the coincidence electronic circuit, programmed a microcontroller to count coincident photons and aligned the optical elements to produce and detect the polarization of the photons.

September 2019 - July 2020

Undergraduate Research Assistant

UB Universitat de Barcelona

Study of ITO nanowires grown on a silicon substrate, co-publishing a paper.

The project took place in the Department of Electronics and Biomedical Engineering of the Universitat de Barcelona and was supervised by Dr. Manel López. We grew Indium Tin Oxide (ITO) on a silicon substrate via electron beam evaporation (EBE) and studied the properties of the nanowires formed. We analyzed the structures created in different initial conditions (changing the atmospheric composition of the chamber, the temperature and the time of exposition). The understanding of the properties of these structures is relevant to the construction of biomedical sensors.
